Italy

Italy is a beautiful country. The people are really welcoming. Outside of Rome, the countryside is beatuiful, the pasta is lifechanging, and you'll never run out of pizza places. There are so many different regions in Italy, each with its own unique culture and cuisine. The country is known for its rich history, stunning architecture, and world-renowned art. Italy offers a diverse range of experiences for travelers. The food is a highlight, with delicious pasta dishes, fresh seafood, and mouthwatering desserts like gelato and tiramisu. Italy is also famous for its wine regions, producing some of the best wines in the world.

Rome

Rome is an amazing city with so many sites to see and visit. From the Parthenon and other ancient roman architecture to the Vatican. The best way to really see it all fast is to set two days to see it all. One to visit all the sites in the city and the other day for the Vatican. The Trevi fountain is easier to see in the morning as the city likes to sleep in and you'll have it all to yourself, but I would book the night tour of the Colluseum to get the full backstage tour and get to go under the structure itself.

Sorrento

Sorrento is a charming coastal town located on the Amalfi Coast. It is known for its stunning views of the Bay of Naples and Mount Vesuvius. Sorrento is famous for its lemon groves and limoncello, a popular Italian liqueur made from lemons. The town has a picturesque historic center with narrow streets, colorful buildings, and beautiful gardens. Sorrento is also a great base for exploring nearby attractions such as Pompeii and the island of Capri. There is also an amazing hike called the path of the Gods which connects a lot of the Amalfi coast towns incoluding Sorrento.

Turin

Turin is a city in northern Italy known for its elegant architecture, rich history, and cultural heritage. It is famous for its baroque buildings, including the Royal Palace of Turin and the Mole Antonelliana, which houses the National Museum of Cinema. Turin is also known for its delicious cuisine, particularly its chocolate and pastries. The city has a vibrant arts scene, with numerous museums, galleries, and theaters. Turin is also home to the famous Shroud of Turin, a religious relic believed by some to be the burial cloth of Jesus Christ. It is super quiant and where my family is from. It is the gateway to a lot of smaller Italian towns in the Piedmontese region.
