Portugal

Portugal was a gorgeous country. The people are really welcoming. Outside of Lisbon and Porto, the countryside is amazing but the people are still very traditionally Catholic and are very communal. The food is amazing and the dollar-to-euro value goes the furthest in Portugal than in any other European Union country. All the cars are manual, but you can still drive on the right side of the road. There are a lot of traditional Portuguese castles and ruins, but also much older Moorish ruins as well.

Lisbon

Lisbon is the capital of Portugal. It is a very hilly city with a lot of old architecture. The people are very friendly and the food is amazing. There is actually the oldest Pasteis de Nata store in the world in Lisbon. The city is very walkable, but there are also trams and buses to help you get around. The nightlife is very lively, and there are many bars and clubs to choose from.

Sintra

Sintra is a small town located just outside of Lisbon. It is known for its beautiful palaces and castles, including the famous Pena Palace and the Moorish Castle. The town is surrounded by lush forests and hills, making it a popular destination for hiking and outdoor activities. Sintra is also a UNESCO World Heritage site, recognized for its cultural significance and stunning architecture.They have these really fun Moped carts that can take you up the mountain to see the sights. The Pena Palace is expensive to enter so be willing to drop about 40 euro to enter.

Porto

Porto is the second-largest city in Portugal, located in the north of the country. It is known for its stunning architecture, historic center, and famous port wine. The city is situated along the Douro River, offering beautiful views and a vibrant waterfront.
